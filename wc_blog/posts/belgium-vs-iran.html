Belgium vs Iran: Red Devils Seek Redemption

Sunday, June 21, 2026

Sunday's match between Belgium and Iran is an early test of character for both sides, with both teams looking to build on their opening day draws. Belgium's 1–1 draw against Egypt may have been seen as a point gained, but Roberto Martinez's men will be eager to push on and make a winning start to their World Cup campaign, while Iran's 2–2 draw with New Zealand might be a slightly more concerning result for Carlos Queiroz's team, given their goalless first half against a New Zealand side that's struggled to find the net.

Belgium's Quest for Redemption

Belgium have been one of the top-ranked teams in the world for a number of years, but they've yet to make it past the quarter-finals at a World Cup since their triumph in 2014. That's a burden they'll be looking to shed quickly, but it's worth noting that their 1–1 draw against Egypt was a decent performance, with Michy Batshuayi's equaliser coming after a spirited first-half display from the Red Devils. If they can build on that, they'll be a force to be reckoned with in this tournament.

Iran's Unlikely Draw

Iran's draw with New Zealand may have been seen as a positive result on paper, given the Kiwis' poor form going into the tournament. However, Iran themselves were largely unimpressive in the first half, and it took a late equaliser from Ali Gholizadeh to salvage a point. Carlos Queiroz's team will be looking to improve their performance significantly in this match, but they face a tough task against a Belgium side that's looking to assert its authority.

The Tactical Battle to Watch

The key area of the pitch in this match could be the left flank, where the pace and trickery of New Zealand's Sarpreet Singh might cause problems for the Belgian left-back Toby Alderweireld. If Iran can find a way to get Singh involved in the game, they might be able to create some opportunities for themselves, but Belgium's midfield trio of Kevin De Bruyne, Axel Witsel, and Youri Tielemans will be looking to nullify any threat and control the tempo of the game.

Belgium are likely to come out on top, given their superior quality and recent form, but Iran won't go down without a fight. Expect a closely contested match, with the Red Devils emerging victorious by a single goal.
