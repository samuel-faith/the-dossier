Egypt Cruise Past New Zealand 3-1 in World Cup Opener

Sunday, June 21, 2026

Full-time result

New Zealand 1 — 3 Egypt

Before kick-off: read our preview of New Zealand vs Egypt — we predicted 0-2.

We predicted a 0-2 win for Egypt, but they ultimately had to work a bit harder than expected to see off New Zealand 3-1. The Pharaohs' superior quality told in the end, but the All Whites will take heart from a spirited performance that saw them grab a consolation goal. As it turned out, our prediction wasn't too far off, but New Zealand's ability to find the back of the net was a pleasant surprise for them, even if it didn't affect the overall outcome.

Egypt's Clinical Finishing

Egypt's victory was built on their clinical finishing, with all three goals showcasing the team's ability to capitalize on scoring opportunities. Their attacking trio caused problems for the New Zealand defense all game, and their interplay was a joy to watch at times. The Egyptians also demonstrated a solid defensive foundation, with their backline holding firm against New Zealand's occasional forays forward.

New Zealand's Brave Effort

New Zealand, on the other hand, can take pride in their bravery and determination. Despite being outclassed by their opponents, they refused to give up and were rewarded with a goal that will give them confidence heading into their next match. Their coach will likely be pleased with the team's overall performance, even if the result didn't go their way. The All Whites' midfield worked tirelessly to try and stem the Egyptian tide, but ultimately, they were unable to cope with the Pharaohs' attacking prowess.

Why It Went This Way

Tactically, Egypt's 4-3-3 formation allowed them to dominate possession and pin New Zealand back. The Egyptians' wide players were able to hug the touchline and provide width, stretching the New Zealand defense and creating opportunities for their fullbacks to make runs into the box. In contrast, New Zealand's 4-2-3-1 formation struggled to cope with Egypt's press, and they often found themselves on the back foot. As the game wore on, Egypt's superior fitness and quality told, and they were able to see out the win with relative ease.

The result leaves Egypt in a strong position in Group G, while New Zealand will need to regroup and refocus ahead of their next match. The All Whites will take heart from their performance, but they know they'll need to improve if they're to have any chance of progressing to the knockout stages. For Egypt, it's a case of building on this momentum and looking to take maximum points from their remaining group games.
