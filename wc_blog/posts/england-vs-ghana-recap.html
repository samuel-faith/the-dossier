England and Ghana Play Out Goalless Draw

Tuesday, June 23, 2026

Full-time result

England 0 — 0 Ghana

Before kick-off: read our preview of England vs Ghana — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a comfortable 2-0 win for England, but it was Ghana who left the pitch with their heads held high after a resolute 0-0 draw. The result will come as a surprise to many, but a closer look at the match reveals that Ghana's disciplined defensive approach and England's lack of cutting edge in the final third were the defining factors.

England's Frustrating Afternoon

England dominated possession for large spells, but their inability to create clear-cut chances was alarming. The team's attacking quartet, which had been touted as one of the most exciting in the tournament, failed to gel, and their usually reliable set-piece threat was neutralized by Ghana's well-drilled defense. England's best opportunities came from range, but their shots lacked the precision to trouble the Ghanaian goalkeeper.

Ghana's Defensive Masterclass

Ghana, on the other hand, executed their game plan to perfection. They set up in a compact 4-5-1 formation, allowing England to have the ball in non-threatening areas before springing into action to win it back. Their midfield five worked tirelessly to close down England's creative outlets, and their back four dealt comfortably with the few crosses that came their way. It was a textbook example of how to nullify a more fancied opponent's attacking threat.

Why It Went This Way

The tactical battle between the two teams was fascinating, with England's 4-3-3 formation struggling to break down Ghana's narrow 4-5-1. England's wide players, usually so effective in stretching defenses, found themselves facing a wall of Ghanaian bodies, and their usual interplay was stifled. Ghana, meanwhile, used their pace on the counter to keep England's defense honest, even if they rarely looked like scoring themselves. The result was a match that was more intriguing than exciting, with neither team able to gain a decisive advantage.

The draw leaves both teams with work to do in Group L, but Ghana will be the happier of the two. They have proven themselves to be a difficult nut to crack, and their point could yet prove valuable in the quest for qualification. England, meanwhile, will need to regroup and find a way to unlock defenses that are set up to frustrate them. With tougher tests to come, they will need to find a solution quickly if they are to fulfill their tournament ambitions.
