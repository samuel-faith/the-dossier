Japan Thrashes Tunisia 4-0 in World Cup 2026 Group F

Sunday, June 21, 2026

Full-time result

Tunisia 0 — 4 Japan

Before kick-off: read our preview of Tunisia vs Japan — we predicted 0-2.

We predicted a 0-2 win for Japan, but they had other plans, handing Tunisia a resounding 4-0 defeat in their World Cup 2026 Group F matchup. The Japanese side's clinical performance on the pitch proved our prediction to be a conservative one, as they outclassed their opponents in every department.

Japan's Dominant Display

Japan's victory was built on a solid foundation of possession football, with their midfield trio of Hidemasa Morishima, Ao Tanaka, and Wataru Endo controlling the tempo of the game. Their forwards, led by the prolific Ayase Ueda, were clinical in their finishing, punished Tunisia's shaky defense at every opportunity. The Japanese side's defense, marshaled by the experienced Maya Yoshida, was equally impressive, restricting Tunisia to just a handful of scoring chances throughout the match.

Tunisia's Disappointing Outing

Tunisia, on the other hand, struggled to cope with Japan's intense pressing and clever movement off the ball. Their usually reliable defense was caught out time and again, with goalkeeper Aymen Dahmen left exposed to deal with the consequences. The Tunisian attack, led by the talented Seifeddine Jaziri, was unable to muster any meaningful threats, with Japan's defense proving to be an impenetrable wall.

Why It Went This Way

The key to Japan's success lay in their ability to exploit Tunisia's narrow midfield, with their wide players, particularly Ritsu Doan and Kaoru Mitoma, causing chaos with their pace and agility. The Japanese side's clever use of space and intelligent movement off the ball also allowed them to create numerous scoring opportunities, which they duly converted with ruthless efficiency. Tunisia, on the other hand, were unable to adapt to Japan's tactics, and their lack of width and creativity in midfield ultimately proved to be their downfall.

The result leaves Japan in a strong position in Group F, while Tunisia will need to regroup and reassess their strategy if they are to have any hope of progressing to the next round. With other teams in the group still to play, the implications of this result are still unfolding, but one thing is certain – Japan have sent a clear message to their rivals with this impressive victory.
