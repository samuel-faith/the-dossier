Argentina Ease Past Austria 2-0 in World Cup 2026

Monday, June 22, 2026

Full-time result

Argentina 2 — 0 Austria

Before kick-off: read our preview of Argentina vs Austria — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a 2-0 win for Argentina, and that's exactly how it played out against Austria in their Group J encounter. The Dossier's forecast looked optimistic at times, but the Albiceleste's quality eventually told, securing a comfortable victory that never really looked in doubt once they took the lead.

Argentina's Dominant Display

Argentina's performance was characterized by their trademark flair, but also a newfound solidity that has been developing under their coach. They controlled the tempo of the game, with their midfield trio dictating the flow of play and creating opportunities for their forwards. The goals, when they came, were a result of this sustained pressure and clever movement off the ball.

Austria's Limited Chances

Austria, on the other hand, struggled to create meaningful chances, often finding themselves on the back foot as Argentina probed for openings. Their best moments came on the counter, but these were few and far between, and they were unable to capitalize on them. As the game wore on, Austria's energy levels dipped, and they became increasingly reliant on long balls, which Argentina's defense dealt with comfortably.

Why It Went This Way

Tactically, Argentina's decision to start with a more defensive-minded midfielder allowed them to protect their back four and launch quick transitions. This caught Austria off guard, as they had expected a more open game. The Austrian coach's attempt to change the dynamic by introducing fresh legs upfront had some impact, but by then, the game was already slipping away. Argentina's ability to adapt and adjust their strategy mid-game was a key factor in their success.

The win puts Argentina in a strong position in Group J, and they will be confident of progressing to the next stage. For Austria, the defeat is a setback, but they still have opportunities to turn their campaign around. As for The Dossier, it's a satisfying result for our prediction model, but the real test comes in the next set of fixtures, where the stakes will be even higher.
