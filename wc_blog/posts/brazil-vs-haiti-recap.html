Brazil Ease Past Haiti 3-0 in World Cup Opener

Friday, June 19, 2026

Full-time result

Brazil 3 — 0 Haiti

Before kick-off: read our preview of Brazil vs Haiti — we predicted 4-0.

We predicted a 4-0 thrashing, but in the end, it was a still-comfortable 3-0 win for Brazil over Haiti in their World Cup 2026 Group C opener. While we weren't quite on the money with the scoreline, the general trend of the match played out very much as expected — Brazil's quality and depth proving too much for their Caribbean opponents to handle. From the outset, it was clear that Brazil had the pace, skill, and cohesion to dictate the terms of the encounter, and they did so with a blend of flair and efficiency.

Brazil's Dominant Display

Brazil's performance was marked by their trademark flair, with their attacking quartet causing constant problems for the Haitian defense. Their movement, interplay, and precision passing allowed them to carve out numerous scoring opportunities, and they took three of them with a combination of individual brilliance and collective cohesion. The Brazilian midfield, meanwhile, controlled the tempo of the game, dictating the flow of play and preventing Haiti from gaining any meaningful foothold in the match.

Haiti's Brave But Futile Efforts

Haiti, to their credit, did not simply roll over and accept their fate. They showed flashes of determination and spirit, particularly in the early stages of the match, and their goalkeeper made a couple of smart saves to deny Brazil further goals. However, their lack of quality and depth ultimately told, as they struggled to cope with Brazil's relentless pressure and precision. Despite their best efforts, they were unable to stem the tide of Brazilian attacks, and they were fortunate to escape without conceding more goals.

Why It Went This Way

The key to Brazil's victory lay in their ability to dominate possession and create scoring opportunities through their clever movement and interplay. Their attacking players were constantly on the move, creating space and confusion among the Haitian defenders, and their midfielders provided the platform for these attacks with their intelligent passing and vision. Haiti, on the other hand, were unable to cope with Brazil's pace and pressure, and they were ultimately overwhelmed by the sheer weight of Brazilian attacks. The gulf in class between the two teams was evident throughout, and the result was never really in doubt from the moment Brazil took the lead.

The win puts Brazil in a strong position in Group C, and they will be confident of progressing to the knockout stages. For Haiti, the defeat is a setback, but they will take heart from their bravery and determination, and they will look to bounce back in their next match. As for Brazil, they will be pleased with the way they started their campaign, but they will know that tougher tests lie ahead — and they will need to maintain their focus and intensity if they are to achieve their ambitions in this tournament.
