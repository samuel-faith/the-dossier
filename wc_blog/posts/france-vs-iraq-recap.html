France Ease Past Iraq 3-0 in World Cup Opener

Monday, June 22, 2026

Full-time result

France 3 — 0 Iraq

Before kick-off: read our preview of France vs Iraq — we predicted 5-0.

We predicted a resounding 5-0 win for France against Iraq, but in the end, it was a more subdued 3-0 victory for the European side. While we were off the mark in terms of the scoreline, the outcome was never really in doubt, as France controlled the tempo of the match from start to finish. The French team's superior quality and tactical prowess ultimately told, as they secured a comfortable win in their World Cup opener.

France's Dominant Display

France's performance was characterized by their usual flair and creativity, with their attacking trio causing problems for the Iraqi defense throughout the match. The French midfield, marshaled by the experienced core, dictated the flow of the game, allowing their forwards to make runs into the box and create scoring opportunities. The defense, meanwhile, was solid and composed, restricting Iraq to few clear chances on goal.

Iraq's Brave But Futile Efforts

Iraq, on the other hand, struggled to cope with France's intensity and pressure. Their defense was often stretched to the limit, and they were unable to contain the French attacks. The Iraqi midfield, despite their best efforts, was unable to gain a foothold in the game, and their forwards were isolated and starved of service. While they showed glimpses of determination and spirit, it was ultimately a tough outing for the Asian side.

Why It Went This Way

Tactically, France's victory was built on their ability to control the center of the park and exploit the wide areas. Their full-backs were given license to push high up the pitch, providing width and stretching the Iraqi defense. This, in turn, created space for the French forwards to make runs into the box and create scoring opportunities. Iraq, on the other hand, were often forced to defend deep and narrow, which limited their ability to launch effective counter-attacks and catch France off guard.

The result means France are off to a strong start in Group I, while Iraq will need to regroup and reassess ahead of their next match. With other teams in the group still to play, it's too early to make any definitive predictions, but France will undoubtedly be one of the teams to watch in the coming weeks. As for Iraq, they'll look to bounce back and cause an upset in their next outing, but for now, they'll have to lick their wounds and learn from this tough defeat.
