Mexico Thrash Czechia 3-0 in World Cup Opener

Wednesday, June 24, 2026

Full-time result

Czechia 0 — 3 Mexico

Before kick-off: read our preview of Czechia vs Mexico — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a 2-0 win for Mexico, but El Tri had other plans, putting three past Czechia in a dominating 3-0 victory. The margin of victory was a surprise, but the outcome was not – Mexico's quality shone through from the start, and they never looked like relinquishing control.

Mexico's Masterclass

Mexico were in charge from the first whistle, their high-intensity press causing Czechia problems from the outset. Their midfield trio of Carlos Rodriguez, Edson Alvarez, and Luis Romo controlled the tempo, allowing their forwards to make runs into the channels and cause chaos in the Czechia defense. The goals, when they came, were a result of this relentless pressure, with each one showcasing a different aspect of Mexico's attacking prowess.

Czechia's Struggles

Czechia, on the other hand, struggled to cope with Mexico's energy and movement. Their defense, which had been solid in the build-up to the tournament, was repeatedly exposed by Mexico's clever interplay and precise passing. The Czechia midfield, usually a strong point, was overrun, and their forwards were starved of service, unable to mount any meaningful attacks.

Why It Went This Way

Tactically, Mexico's decision to start with a front three, rather than their usual two, paid dividends. The extra man up front allowed them to pin Czechia's defense back and create opportunities through sheer weight of numbers. Czechia, meanwhile, will rue their inability to adjust to Mexico's press, which consistently won the ball back in dangerous areas and launched counter-attacks.

The result leaves Mexico in a strong position in Group A, while Czechia will need to regroup and reassess ahead of their next fixture. With this performance, Mexico have sent a statement to the rest of the tournament – they are a force to be reckoned with, and will not be easy to beat.
