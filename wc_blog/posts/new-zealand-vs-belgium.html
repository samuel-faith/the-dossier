Belgium Seek First Win Against New Zealand

Friday, June 26, 2026

In a Group G clash that could significantly impact the standings, Belgium are set to face New Zealand on Friday, June 26. With both teams still searching for their first win in the tournament, the pressure is on to secure three crucial points. Belgium, despite being favourites, have had a slow start, while New Zealand have shown resilience in their opening matches.

Belgium's Quest for Momentum

Belgium's campaign so far has been marked by draws against Egypt and Iran. Despite their strong squad, they have struggled to find the winning formula. Their midfield and attack, packed with talent, need to click into gear if they are to break down New Zealand's defense and secure a much-needed victory. The Belgian side is known for its technical prowess and tactical flexibility, which they will likely rely on to outmaneuver their opponents.

New Zealand's Underdog Spirit

New Zealand, on the other hand, has shown a commendable spirit in their matches against Iran and Egypt. Although they have only managed a draw and a loss, their performances have been full of heart and determination. They will need to draw upon this resilience to counter Belgium's attacking flair and potentially catch them off guard with swift counterattacks.

The Tactical Battle to Watch

The midfield battle will be crucial in this encounter. Belgium's ability to control the tempo of the game through their midfield maestros will be pitted against New Zealand's industrious and physical approach. If Belgium can dominate this area, they should be able to create enough scoring opportunities to outscore their opponents. However, if New Zealand can disrupt Belgium's rhythm and transition quickly, they might find themselves with opportunities to snatch a result.

Given the current form and the historical context, Belgium's quality and depth should eventually tell, leading them to a hard-fought but deserved victory over New Zealand.
