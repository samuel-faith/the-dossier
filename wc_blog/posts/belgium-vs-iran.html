Belgium Face Iran in Group G Opener

Sunday, June 21, 2026

The 2026 World Cup is finally underway, and one of the most intriguing group stage matches is about to kick off. Belgium, a team often touted as dark horses for the tournament, will face off against Iran in their Group G opener. With both teams looking to make a statement, this match promises to be an exciting clash of styles.

Belgium's Golden Generation

Belgium's squad is filled with experienced players who have been in this situation before. Their golden generation, led by the likes of Kevin De Bruyne and Eden Hazard, will be looking to make the most of this opportunity. With their quick passing and movement, they can be a nightmare to defend against, and Iran will need to be at the top of their game to keep them at bay.

Iran's Counter-Attacking Threat

Iran, on the other hand, will be looking to use their pace on the counter-attack to catch Belgium off guard. With their athletic wingers and target man up front, they have the potential to cause problems for the Belgian defense. However, they will need to be disciplined and organized at the back to prevent Belgium's attacking talent from running riot.

The Tactical Battle to Watch

The key battle in this match will be in the midfield area, where Belgium's creative trio of De Bruyne, Hazard, and Axel Witsel will look to dominate possession and create scoring opportunities. Iran, meanwhile, will rely on their destroyer, Saeid Ezatolahi, to break up the play and launch quick counter-attacks. If Belgium can find a way to nullify Ezatolahi's influence, they should be able to control the tempo of the game.

Given the talent at their disposal, Belgium should have enough to see off the challenge of Iran, and a 2-0 win for the Red Devils is the most likely outcome.
