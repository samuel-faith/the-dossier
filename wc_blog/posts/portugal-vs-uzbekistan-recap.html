Portugal Thrashes Uzbekistan 5-0 in World Cup 2026

Tuesday, June 23, 2026

Full-time result

Portugal 5 — 0 Uzbekistan

Before kick-off: read our preview of Portugal vs Uzbekistan — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a 2-0 win for Portugal, but it turned out to be a much more one-sided affair as they thrashed Uzbekistan 5-0 in their Group K clash. The margin of victory was a surprise, but the outcome was not – Portugal's quality and depth ultimately told as they outclassed their opponents in every department.

Portugal's Dominant Display

From the outset, Portugal looked like a team on a mission. They moved the ball with purpose and precision, creating chance after chance as Uzbekistan struggled to cope with their opponents' pace and movement. The Portuguese attack was led by their talented forwards, who caused chaos in the Uzbekistan defense with their clever interplay and clinical finishing. As the game wore on, Portugal's dominance only grew, with their midfielders and defenders joining in on the act to create a barrage of scoring opportunities.

Uzbekistan's Struggles

Uzbekistan, on the other hand, found it difficult to get a foothold in the game. Their defense was breached time and again, and their attack was unable to muster any meaningful threats against the Portuguese goal. The Uzbekistan players looked tired and disjointed, unable to match the intensity and energy of their opponents. As the scoreline grew, so did the sense of despair among the Uzbekistan players, who were unable to stem the tide of Portuguese attacks.

Why It Went This Way

The gulf in class between the two teams was evident from the start, with Portugal's superior technique, tactics, and athleticism proving too much for Uzbekistan to handle. The Portuguese team's ability to press high up the pitch and win the ball back quickly caught Uzbekistan off guard, allowing them to launch wave after wave of attacks. Uzbekistan's inability to cope with Portugal's press and their failure to create any meaningful counter-attacks sealed their fate, as they were unable to relieve the pressure on their defense.

The result leaves Portugal in a strong position in Group K, while Uzbekistan will need to regroup and reassess their strategy if they are to have any chance of progressing to the next stage. As for Portugal, they will look to build on this impressive performance and take momentum into their upcoming matches, where they will face stiffer challenges but will be confident of their ability to overcome them.
