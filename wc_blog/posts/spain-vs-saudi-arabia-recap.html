Spain Thrash Saudi Arabia 4-0 in World Cup Opener

Sunday, June 21, 2026

Full-time result

Spain 4 — 0 Saudi Arabia

Before kick-off: read our preview of Spain vs Saudi Arabia — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a 2-0 win for Spain, but they had other plans, thrashing Saudi Arabia 4-0 in their World Cup opener. The margin of victory was surprising, but the outcome was not — Spain's class and experience ultimately told, as they outplayed their opponents in every department. The scoreline was a fair reflection of the gulf between the two teams, with Spain's midfield dominance and clinical finishing proving too much for Saudi Arabia to handle.

Spain's Dominant Display

Spain's performance was a masterclass in possession football, with their midfield trio of Pedri, Gavi, and Rodri controlling the tempo of the game. They suffocated Saudi Arabia's attacks, winning the ball back quickly and transitioning seamlessly into counter-attacks. The front three of Olmo, Morata, and Williams were clinical in their finishing, taking advantage of the chances created by their teammates. Spain's coach will be delighted with the way his team executed their game plan, and the scoreline was a testament to their superiority.

Saudi Arabia's Struggles

Saudi Arabia, on the other hand, struggled to cope with Spain's intensity and pressing. They were unable to maintain possession for any prolonged period, and their attacks were few and far between. Their defense was exposed time and again, and they were unable to deal with the movement and interplay of Spain's front three. The scoreline could have been even worse for Saudi Arabia, as Spain created several other clear-cut chances throughout the game. Saudi Arabia's coach will need to go back to the drawing board and rethink his team's strategy if they are to have any chance of progressing from the group.

Why It Went This Way

The key to Spain's victory was their ability to dominate the midfield battle. They won the physical and tactical battle, with their midfield trio outmuscling and outmaneuvering their Saudi Arabian counterparts. This allowed them to control the tempo of the game and create chances at will. Saudi Arabia, on the other hand, were unable to cope with the pressure and intensity of Spain's play, and they were ultimately overwhelmed. The gulf in class and experience between the two teams was evident, and Spain's victory was a deserved one.

The result leaves Spain top of Group H, with a significant goal difference advantage over their rivals. Saudi Arabia, on the other hand, will need to regroup and refocus if they are to have any chance of progressing from the group. The match was a statement of intent from Spain, and they will be confident of making a deep run in the tournament. For Saudi Arabia, it's back to the drawing board — they need to find a way to compete with the top teams in the group if they are to have any chance of success.
