Netherlands Thrash Sweden 5-1 in World Cup 2026 Group F

Saturday, June 20, 2026

Full-time result

Netherlands 5 — 1 Sweden

Before kick-off: read our preview of Netherlands vs Sweden — we predicted 2-0.

We predicted a comfortable 2-0 win for the Netherlands, but they had other plans, demolishing Sweden 5-1 in a one-sided Group F encounter. The Dutch were always in control, and their clinical finishing made the difference, but it's fair to say our prediction was on the conservative side. As it turned out, Sweden's struggles to contain the Netherlands' attacking flair proved costly, and the final scoreline was a fair reflection of the proceedings.

Netherlands' Attacking Masterclass

The Netherlands' victory was built on a solid foundation of pace, movement, and precision passing, which consistently stretched the Swedish defense. Their front line, in particular, was a constant thorn in Sweden's side, with clever interplay and intelligent runs causing chaos in the Swedish backline. The Dutch midfield, meanwhile, dominated possession, dictating the tempo of the game and starving Sweden of the ball. It was a performance that will have delighted their coach and sent a statement to their Group F rivals.

Sweden's Disappointing Display

For Sweden, this was a sobering defeat, one that exposed their defensive vulnerabilities and highlighted their struggles to cope with top-level opposition. They were second best in almost every department, and their usually reliable defense was breached time and again by the Netherlands' razor-sharp attack. The Swedish midfield, usually the engine room of their team, was outmuscled and outmaneuvered, leaving their defense exposed and their attack starved of supply. It's back to the drawing board for the Swedes, who must regroup and refocus ahead of their next Group F fixture.

Why It Went This Way

Tactically, the Netherlands' flexibility and adaptability proved decisive, as they seamlessly switched between different formations and systems to keep Sweden guessing. Their use of width, in particular, was a key factor, with their wingers consistently pinning back the Swedish full-backs and creating space for their attacking midfielders to exploit. Sweden, on the other hand, were too rigid and predictable, sticking to their usual 4-4-2 formation and failing to adjust to the Netherlands' clever movement and interplay. It was a tale of two teams, one with a clear plan and the execution to match, the other struggling to cope with the pace and precision of their opponents.

As the Group F table begins to take shape, the Netherlands' resounding win has sent a clear message to their rivals. They are a force to be reckoned with, and their attacking prowess will make them a difficult opponent for anyone in the tournament. For Sweden, the defeat is a setback, but not a disaster – they still have time to recover and push for a place in the knockout stages. The battle for Group F supremacy is heating up, and the next round of fixtures promises to be just as intriguing.
