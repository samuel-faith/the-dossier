Belgium and Iran Play Out Goalless Draw

Sunday, June 21, 2026

Full-time result

Belgium 0 — 0 Iran

Before kick-off: read our preview of Belgium vs Iran — we predicted 1-0.

We predicted a narrow 1-0 win for Belgium, but in the end, neither side could find the breakthrough as the match finished 0-0. While our prediction was off the mark, the tight and competitive nature of the encounter was very much in line with expectations. Both teams created chances, but a combination of solid defending and a lack of clinical finishing meant that the stalemate was never really threatened.

Belgium's Frustrating Evening

Belgium dominated possession for large spells of the match, but their inability to create clear-cut chances was a major concern. Their attacking quartet of De Bruyne, Hazard, Lukaku, and Carrasco all had glimpses of quality, but Iran's well-organized defense was equal to the task. The Belgian players will be disappointed with the lack of penetration in their play, and the fact that they didn't test the Iranian goalkeeper enough will be a worry for Roberto Martinez.

Iran's Resolute Performance

Iran, on the other hand, can take great heart from their performance. They set up to absorb pressure and hit Belgium on the counter, and while they didn't create a plethora of chances, they always looked a threat when they broke forward. Their defense was resolute, and the goalkeeper had a solid game, making a few crucial saves to deny Belgium. Carlos Queiroz's team will feel that they deserved their point, and they can take confidence from the fact that they were able to nullify Belgium's attacking threat.

Why It Went This Way

The main reason for the 0-0 draw was the fact that both teams cancelled each other out tactically. Belgium's attacking flair was matched by Iran's defensive solidity, and neither side could find a way to break the deadlock. The match was also notable for the lack of space in midfield, with both teams packing the center of the park and denying each other time and space to create. As a result, the game became a war of attrition, with both teams waiting for the other to make a mistake rather than taking the initiative themselves.

The draw leaves both teams with work to do in Group G, but it's a result that will suit Iran more than Belgium. The Belgians need to find a way to break down stubborn defenses if they're to make a deep run in the tournament, while Iran can take heart from the fact that they're still very much in the mix. With other teams in the group still to play, it's too early to make any definitive predictions, but one thing is for sure - this group is going to be tightly contested.
