Norway vs Senegal: Can Senegal bounce back?

Monday, June 22, 2026

Norway's 4–1 thrashing of Iraq has set them up for a challenging Group I, where they will face Senegal on Monday. Norway's attacking prowess was on full display, with their clinical finishers making the most of their chances. They will be looking to build on this momentum and take control of their group.

Norway's Form

Norway's attack is led by Martin Eide, who scored two goals against Iraq and has been in impressive form. Their midfield, featuring the experienced duo of Larsen and Jensen, provides a solid foundation for their attacking play. However, they have struggled defensively, conceding four goals in their opening match, and will need to improve their defensive solidity to take their chances.

Senegal's Road to Recovery

Senegal's 3–1 loss to France has put them under pressure in Group I, but they have the quality to bounce back. Their attack, featuring the pacey Ismaila Sarr, has the potential to cause problems for Norway's defense. However, they will need to tighten up their defense and prevent Norway from taking control of the game.

The Tactical Battle to Watch

The key to this match will be how Norway's defense copes with Senegal's pacey attackers. If Norway can absorb the pressure and prevent Senegal from creating clear-cut chances, they will be well on their way to taking all three points. However, if Senegal can find a way to break down Norway's defense, they will have a real chance of taking a crucial point.

Norway's attacking prowess should see them edge out Senegal in a close encounter, with a 2–0 victory predicted.
